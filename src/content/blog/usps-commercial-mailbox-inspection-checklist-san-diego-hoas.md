- HOA boards replacing outdated mailbox clusters.

## Pre-inspection priorities at a glance
Before scheduling inspection/sign-off, confirm:
- Location has been coordinated with USPS.
- Carrier approach and delivery access are clear.
- Pad, anchors, and mounting match equipment requirements.
- ADA clearance and approach conditions are verified.
- Parcel locker counts align with resident package demand.
- Tenant communication and key handoff plans are ready.

## 1) Location approval and USPS coordination
Inspection issues often begin with location assumptions. Coordinate with USPS before field work is finalized.

**Checklist**
- Share proposed mailbox location(s), photos, and layout notes.
- Confirm carrier approach path and delivery direction.
- Document any required location or orientation changes.
- Keep written records of coordination milestones for board files.

## 2) Carrier access and daily delivery functionality
A site can look good for residents but still fail operationally for delivery. Build your checklist around daily carrier use.

**Checklist**
- Delivery vehicle access is unobstructed during normal mail windows.
- Carrier standing/work area is level and free of hazards.
- Gates, bollards, and landscaping do not block service paths.
- Lighting and visibility support early morning delivery conditions.

## 3) Concrete pads, anchoring, and mounting readiness
Mailbox security and long-term performance depend on foundation quality.

**Checklist**
- Pad dimensions and reinforcement match specified equipment.
- Cure times are observed before final mounting.
- Anchors align with manufacturer requirements.
- Unit is level, secure, and stable under normal use.

Use only USPS-approved equipment appropriate for centralized delivery applications and project scope.

## 4) ADA clearances and resident approach paths
Accessibility should be verified in the field, not assumed from drawings.

**Checklist**
- Clear approach path is stable and unobstructed.
- Reach and maneuvering clearances are validated at installed height.
- Surface slope and transitions do not create access barriers.
- Adjacent elements (curbs, planters, signage) do not reduce usable clearance.

## 5) Parcel locker planning before sign-off
Package volume is now a major operational factor for HOAs.

**Checklist**
- Locker quantity aligns with current package demand.
- Locker placement does not compromise ADA approach space.
- Management has a plan for overflow periods and resident communication.

## 6) Tenant communication and key handoff plan
Resident confusion can undermine an otherwise successful installation.

**Checklist**
- Notice schedule is sent before installation and before go-live.
- Temporary mail access instructions are clear if service is interrupted.
- Key assignment list is prepared and verified by management.
- Move-in/move-out key control process is documented.

## 7) Final walkthrough and inspection/sign-off package
A structured walkthrough helps boards close the project cleanly.

**Checklist**
- Physical installation matches approved location and orientation.
- Compartments, parcel lockers, and doors operate correctly.
- Site is clean, safe, and ready for resident use.
- Sign-off documents and handoff records are stored for HOA files.

## Common issues that delay sign-off
- Location moved in the field without re-coordination.
- Carrier access blocked by gates or parked vehicles.
- Pad or anchor installation incomplete at inspection time.
- ADA clearances reduced by nearby site features.
- No documented plan for key distribution.

## Frequently asked questions
**Do HOA mailbox projects always require USPS coordination?**
For centralized delivery projects, boards should coordinate with USPS on placement, access, and inspection/sign-off expectations before final installation.

**Can we guarantee approval if we keep the old mailbox location?**
No. Existing locations still need to be reviewed against current delivery and access requirements.

**What should we bring to the final walkthrough?**
Bring location notes, access confirmations, installation scope details, and the key handoff list so management and installers can close open items quickly.
